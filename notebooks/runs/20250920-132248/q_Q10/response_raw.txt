### Response to Q10

Arguments supporting the notion of agents not being solely self-interested include:

- The concept of altruism, which suggests individuals may help others due to feelings of obligation or anticipated future benefits (p.50). 

Key text from the article supporting this includes:

- "Altruism can be reciprocity-driven, where individuals help others because they feel obligated to return a favor..." (p.50). 

This indicates that motivations can extend beyond self-interest.
